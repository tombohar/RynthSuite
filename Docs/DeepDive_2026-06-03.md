# RynthAi — Thorough Deep Dive (2026-06-03, v0.20)

**Scope:** Full read-only audit of `Plugins\RynthCore.Plugin.RynthAi` (~44.6k LOC) against the
*current* code at v0.20. Produced from a 6-agent subsystem sweep + direct verification of the
load-bearing claims. **Supersedes the priority ordering of `DeepDive_MasterPlan_2026-05-16.md`**
(that doc is 17 days / 7 releases stale — most of its P0/P1 items are now fixed; see §2).

**Strategic decision (user, 2026-06-03):** improvements land in the **original RynthAi**
(`C:\Projects\RynthSuite`), not the RynthBot/RC2 port. v0.20 shipped here 2 days ago and the
crash class that drove the port is closed, so the original is the active, daily-driven target.
§7 records what to carry to RC2 later.

---

## 0. Verdict

RynthAi has **won the solo-grind loop** — combat, buffing, looting, salvage, navigation, and a
~250-function VTank Meta engine are all mature and ACE-hardened. The last ~7 releases were almost
entirely combat-correctness-on-ACE, and it shows: combat/buff are battle-tested; nav/loot/salvage/meta
are mature-with-known-gaps. Nearly every crash-class and correctness item from the May audit is
**genuinely fixed**. Two things now hold the product back, and they are the whole story:

1. **The orchestration layer is half-migrated and fights itself** — the Activity Arbiter stalled at
   Step 2 of 5. This is the documented *"bot just stands there"* freeze class. (§3, P0.)
2. **It is entirely single-character.** No multibox coordination, no vendor/trade, no death recovery
   — exactly where a daily multiboxer feels the pain. (§4, Tier 1.)

## 3. Improvements (prioritized)

### P0 — Close the freeze class: finish (or kill) the Activity Arbiter

**Evidence the migration stalled at Step 2/5:**
- `ActivityArbiter` class header still reads *"STEP 1 (shadow mode): only DECIDES and LOGS"*
  (`ActivityArbiter.cs:7`) — never updated.
- `ApplyStep2` is authoritative **only** for `Combat`/`Navigating`/`Default`
  (`ActivityArbiter.cs:142-148`); it explicitly **defers to legacy** for Buffing/Looting/Salvaging
  (`:137-140`).
- Its `Current` enum decision (`:109,128`) is computed every tick and **read by nothing** — behavior
  is still keyed off the stringly-typed `settings.BotAction`, not the typed decision.
- Its own comment claims *"sole writer of Navigating"* (`:120-123`) but **`NavigationEngine.cs:219`
  also writes `"Navigating"`** — the single-writer invariant it relies on is already violated.
- The OnTick body (`RynthAiPlugin.cs:518-756`) is ~240 lines of legacy string-cascade + **2 gap-fills**
  that re-introduce multi-writer behavior (`:542-551` buffing, `:611-630` salvage) + **4 hand-managed
  pause flags** (`_buffingPausedNav/_combatPausedNav/_corpsePausedNav/_combatEndedAt`, `:558-566,704-746`)
  + a `LootGraceMs` grace timer.
- **~19 functional `BotAction` writes across 5 files** (BuffManager 7, CorpseOpenController 4,
  RynthAiPlugin gap-fills 4, NavigationEngine 1, arbiter 1; +UI resets in LegacyDashboardRenderer/
  LegacyNavigationUi). The May plan counted 17 "remaining"; the real number went **up**.

**This is the root cause of "bot stands there doing nothing":** any of the 19 writers (or a stale
pause flag) can leave `BotAction` in a state that blocks the others, and there's no single authority
to recompute the truth.

**Recommended fix — Option A (finish the migration), staged, test between each:**
Make the typed `Decide()` decision authoritative and reduce the string to a *display projection*.
1. **Step 3 (Buffing):** drop `"Buffing"` from `legacyOwnsString` (`:138`); delete the OnTick buff
   gap-fill (`:542-551`) and buff-pause block (`:555-570`); delete the 7 BuffManager writes; move
   "pending cast holds Buffing" into the **pure predicate** (`WantBuffing || _pendingSpellId != 0`).
   **Keep Buffing at top priority** (survival).
2. **Step 4 (Loot+Salvage):** make the arbiter authoritative for `Looting`/`Salvaging`; delete the
   salvage gap-fill (`:611-630`) and the 4 CorpseOpenController writes; add pure `HasLootWork`.
3. **Step 5 (delete cascade):** delete the 4 pause flags + `_combatEndedAt`/`LootGraceMs`; collapse
   OnTick to `inputs → Decide → switch(Current){ winner.Tick() } → always Think()`; the string becomes
   `ToBotAction(Current)` written once for the UI.

Option B (kill the class) is **not recommended** — it leaves the freeze class intact.
**Effort: L** (but the value is the single highest in the codebase; do it one step per test session).

> **Bundle the BoostLootPriority hatch (below) into Step 4** — it disappears when the arbiter owns Looting.

### P3 — Observability & latent crash-class

- **No `GetStateSnapshot()` for Salvage or Nav** (Combat/Buff/Meta have one). When they wedge you're
  guessing. Add snapshots exposing phase/queue/last-error. **S each.**
- **No "why" view.** The arbiter already computes `ArbiterInputs` every tick (`RynthAiPlugin.cs:530-537`)
  — expose it as `/ra why`. **S, high diagnostic value.**
- **`.dat` geometry caches are plain `Dictionary` loaded on a ThreadPool thread but read game-thread**
  (`GeometryLoader.cs:36-52`, `DungeonLOS.cs:29-50`; init at `RynthAiPlugin.cs:200`). The
  game-thread-only assumption is violated by the loader thread → the off-thread-mutation AV class.
  Gate `.dat` access to the game thread or `ConcurrentDictionary` + lock evictions. **M.**
- **`FlushLandblock` misses the DungeonLOS + DungeonPathfinder caches** — stale walls/graph persist
  after a dungeon change. Fan the flush out. **S.**
- **`ReadGameClock` raw `Marshal.ReadInt64(0x008379A8)` with no SEH guard** — any `getgame*` expr
  pre-world-entry can AV. Wrap in the engine SEH trampoline. **M.**
- **`AcStubs.cs` still serves faked skill/spell reads** as fallback (the "skills read 0" artifact);
  ~25 quality-read sites remain ungated (May P0-2). Structural SEH trampoline is the real fix. **L.**

## 5. Cross-cutting themes

- **Observability is uneven** — Salvage + Nav lack the `GetStateSnapshot()` the others have; there's
  no "what is the bot doing and why" surface despite the data being computed.
- **Single-character worldview is baked in** — nothing models "my other accounts," which is *the*
  recurring blocker behind every Tier-1 feature.
- **The off-thread AC-read crash class is contained, not eliminated** — selection-clobber closed (v0.20),
  but `.dat` caches and ~25 quality reads remain; SEH trampoline is the durable fix.
- **Hardcoded fragility** — DAT path `C:\Games\RynthCore\AcClient`; some fixed RVAs without
  signature-scan (build-drift fragile).
- **No automated tests** — every change is verified by live soak. A handful of pure-logic units
  (`Decide()`, `.utl` evaluator, expression engine, salvage band math) would be cheap and high-value.
- **In-AC settings split** — several salvage/advanced settings live only in the ImGui
  `LegacyAdvancedSettingsUi`; no Avalonia parity, so they vanish when ImGui retires.

## 7. Notes for the eventual RynthBot / RC2 port

When this work eventually moves to RC2:
- **Port the *typed* arbiter, not the string cascade.** RC2 is the chance to start with `Decide()`
  authoritative and never reintroduce `BotAction`-as-truth. This doc's §3 P0 is the blueprint.
- The **coordination bus** should be designed cross-process from day one — RC2's two-process model
  (C++ shim + .NET host) makes an IPC bus more natural than in-process RynthAi.
- Carry the **fixed** list (§2) forward as "already-solved" so the port doesn't re-derive them.
- Combat's explicit-target + empirical-tier model is the crown jewel — port it wholesale.
